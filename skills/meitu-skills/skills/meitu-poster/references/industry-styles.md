## Mandatory Layout Rules for 6 Special Industries

For: primary school / kindergarten, university / high school, medical clinic, nonprofit organization, finance, rental housing / car rental — when illustration is NOT triggered, design directions must use appropriate combinations from these 3 fixed layout rules:

- **Layout Rule 1**: Vertical photo-hero poster with brand-color tone-on-tone gradient overlay panel carrying stacked title and fixed information hierarchy
- **Layout Rule 2**: Circle photo cutouts, soft minimal layout
- **Layout Rule 3**: Upper–lower regional split with gradient color information panel

Layout rules must be strongly bound to the corresponding industry's matched styles, fonts, and composition characteristics.

## Industry Mapping Table

### 1. Bar / Nightclub / Music

- **Styles:** Product Photography Aesthetic, New American Retro, Gothic Vintage, Hazy Glow Nightclub Photography, Luxury Velvet Photography, Futuristic Retro Acid Liquid Gold Aesthetic
- **Fonts:** Bold Geometric Sans, Shadow / 3D Font, Futuristic Sans, Retro Serif, Handwritten Script
- **Composition:** Diagonal dynamic, scattered dynamic layouts, large visuals + cut lines
- **Feel:** Power, dynamism, trendiness, fashion

### 2. Food & Beverage / Coffee / Bakery

- **Styles:** New American Retro, Dopamine Decor, Nordic Minimal, Etching Illustration, Product Photography Aesthetic, American Comic Style
- **Fonts:** Rounded Friendly, Handwritten Script, Hand-painted, Bold Sans-Serif
- **Composition:** Center composition + editorial white-space (large product visuals with text blocks)
- **Feel:** Warm, refined, "makes you want a bite"

### 3. Beauty / Skincare / Hair / Nails

- **Styles:** Product Photography Aesthetic, Earthy Organic Aesthetic, Dopamine Decor, Luxury Photography Style
- **Fonts:** Serif (Didot, Garamond), Minimal Sans-Serif (Helvetica, Avenir), Handwritten Decorative

- **Feel:** Bright soft lighting, warm earth tones or fresh pinks, fine texture

### 4. Festivals / Events

- **Fonts:** Handwritten Script, Decorative / Ornamented, Retro Serif

## Unknown Industry Handling

When the identified industry is NOT in the table above, auto-adapt:

**Output structure:**
- **Matched styles**: TOP matching styles, formatted as "Core Style – {Industry} Adaptation Variant" (e.g., Product Photography Aesthetic – Pet Medical Adaptation Variant)
- **Font recommendations**: Based on core semantic features, formatted as "Font type (usage scenario)" (e.g., Minimal Sans-Serif (body text), Rounded Friendly (headlines))
- **Composition characteristics**: Combine functional type + industry scenarios, extract 3–4 core composition logics
- **Recommended visual feel**: Extract 4 core sensations from emotional tone + market preference

**Constraint rules:**
1. **Style binding** — All styles must bind to unknown industry's core semantic features. Style–industry disconnection prohibited.
2. **Medium prohibition** — Follow negative lexicon strictly. No watercolor/line/etching when illustration not triggered.
3. **Differentiation** — Design direction must form clear, stable, recognizable characteristics in color, composition, lighting.
4. **Market adaptation** — Styles must align with target market (Europe → low saturation + minimalist; North America → high contrast + dynamic).
5. **Quality fallback** — If quality requirements not met after 3 iterations → use universal safe combination: Product Photography Aesthetic + Editorial Minimal + Nordic Minimal + Lifestyle Photography. Label "manual optimization of style adaptation required."
